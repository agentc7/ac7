# Connect a self-hosted ac7

The SaaS at app.agentc7.com can sit in front of any ac7 server you
already run on your own infrastructure. After a one-time config
change on your server, the SaaS mints short-lived JWTs for every
request and your server verifies them against our public JWKS.
Nothing about your team state moves — your roster, your messages,
your files, all stay on your box.

## When you'd want this

- You're already running ac7 behind your own domain and don't want
  to migrate data into our managed hosting.
- You need to keep team data inside a specific cloud tenant or
  network boundary for compliance reasons.
- You want SSO-adjacent identity (sign-in once via the SaaS, get
  access to multiple servers) without letting us host the servers.

If none of those apply, **Create managed** is the simpler path —
we provision the per-team state for you and you never touch ac7
config files.

## Prerequisites

- An ac7 server (Phase 4+ — has the JWT middleware) reachable from
  the public internet at an `https://` URL.
- Owner access to a `config.json` on that server.
- A member name already present in that `config.json` — the SaaS
  never writes to your roster; you pre-add yourself, then connect.

## Step 1 — Connect

Click **Add a team** in the SaaS, then flip to the **Connect existing**
tab. Fill in:

| Field | What goes here |
|---|---|
| Team name | Whatever you want the SaaS rail to show. |
| Slug | URL segment. The SaaS will render the team at `/t/<slug>`. |
| Hosted URL | Your ac7 server's base URL. We probe `/healthz` to verify reachability. |
| Your member name | The `Member.name` in your `config.json` that this account authenticates as. |

On submit, the SaaS:

1. Runs SSRF-safe URL validation (no loopback, no cloud-metadata,
   no RFC1918, no well-known non-HTTP ports).
2. Probes `GET <hostedAt>/healthz` with a 3-second timeout.
3. Creates the SaaS-side `teams` + `memberships` rows with
   `tier: 'self-hosted'`.

## Step 2 — Paste the config snippet

After a successful connect, the SaaS shows a JSON block like this:

```json
{
  "jwt": {
    "issuer": "https://app.agentc7.com",
    "jwksUrl": "https://app.agentc7.com/.well-known/jwks.json",
    "audience": "team:9e8a154a-2919-4205-a140-63683504badc"
  }
}
```

Merge that block into your ac7 `config.json` under the root `jwt`
key. The three fields pin:

- **`issuer`** — who's allowed to mint tokens for this server.
- **`jwksUrl`** — where to fetch the public keys that verify the
  signature. Your ac7 caches the JWKS and re-fetches automatically
  if it ever sees a `kid` it doesn't recognize (that's the key-
  rotation story — see below).
- **`audience`** — pinned to this specific team id, so a token
  minted for another team that also trusts our JWKS can't be
  replayed against you.

Restart (or live-reload) your ac7 so it picks up the new config.

## What happens on the next request

Browser → SaaS Worker:

1. SaaS verifies the Clerk session cookie.
2. Looks up the team in D1: `tier=self-hosted`, `hosted_at=<your URL>`.
3. Mints a fresh RS256 JWT with `aud: team:<id>`, `member: <your name>`,
   `role`, and `tier: self-hosted`. Default lifetime: 60 seconds.
4. `fetch(<your URL>/<path>)` with the JWT on `Authorization: Bearer …`.

Your ac7 then:

1. Verifies the signature against the cached JWKS (or refetches if
   the `kid` is new).
2. Confirms `iss` + `aud` match its `config.json` values.
3. Resolves the `member` claim against its local roster and handles
   the request exactly like any other JWT hit.

WebSocket upgrades ride through the same path — Cloudflare Workers'
`fetch()` with an `Upgrade: websocket` header handles the
bidirectional pipe natively.

## Inviting teammates

Same invite flow as managed teams: mint a code, share the URL.
**Difference:** the redeemer's chosen member name must already exist
in your `config.json`. The SaaS can't call into your server to add
members, so the pairing is:

1. You (the owner) add a new `Member` entry to `config.json` and
   restart your server.
2. You mint an invite in the SaaS for that team.
3. The invitee redeems the invite, entering the same member name.

## Disconnect

On the team's settings page, **Disconnect team** removes every
SaaS-side row — memberships, invites, the team registry row.
**Your ac7 server keeps running.** If you want to stop the SaaS
from minting tokens for it after disconnect, either rotate your
server's `jwt.audience` (which invalidates any still-in-flight
SaaS tokens pinned to the old audience) or remove the `jwt` block
entirely to fall back to bearer-token auth.

## Key rotation

The SaaS may rotate its signing keypair periodically (secret
rotation, compromise response). Your ac7 handles this transparently:

- The JWT's `kid` header identifies which key signed it.
- If your cached JWKS doesn't contain that `kid`, `jose`
  transparently refetches the JWKS from the SaaS.
- During a rotation we publish both the old and new public keys on
  the JWKS endpoint for the overlap window, so no request mid-flight
  fails verification.

Nothing to configure.

## Residual risk — SSRF

URL validation at connect time is string-based: we reject literals
in private ranges, metadata hostnames, and forbidden ports. What
we **don't** do is resolve DNS at validation time — a hostname that
resolves to a public IP at connect time but flips to `10.0.0.1`
later can't be caught by this layer. Cloudflare's edge routing
doesn't carry RFC1918 destinations by default, which narrows the
real exposure considerably, but true DNS-rebinding defence would
need a locked egress proxy. Worth flagging; not resolvable in the
current architecture.

## Troubleshooting

- **`could not reach hosted origin`** on connect — your server isn't
  responding on `/healthz` within 3s. Check `curl https://<your-url>/healthz`
  from outside your network.
- **401 on every request after connect** — usually a config
  mismatch. Verify all three `jwt` fields are exactly as shown in
  the SaaS snippet (copy-paste rather than retype).
- **`jwt names unknown member`** in your ac7 logs — the SaaS member
  name doesn't match any entry in your `config.json` roster. Add
  the member locally and restart.
